ABA SERVICE AGREEMENT AND CONSENT FORM
Consent for Services and Use of Insurance

By signing this agreement, I authorize NY Special Care, Inc. to provide Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) services to my child/dependent. I also authorize NY Special Care to bill my insurance provider directly for services rendered. I understand:

  • I must provide current, accurate insurance information and notify NY Special Care of any changes.
  • I am responsible for any applicable copayments, deductibles, or services not covered by insurance.
  • Protected Health Information (PHI) may be shared with insurance companies for the purposes of billing and authorization.
Services Offered
  • ABA services are provided by Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs), Licensed Behavior Analysts (LBAs), and/or Registered Behavior Technicians (RBTs) under supervision.
  • Services include initial assessments, individualized treatment planning, direct therapy, parent training, data collection, and ongoing clinical supervision.
  • Treatment is based on evidence-based practices and follows the ethical standards of the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB).
  • ABA therapy may take place in the home, clinic, community, or via telehealth, based on the family’s needs and insurance authorization.
Parent/Caregiver Participation
  • Caregiver involvement is a critical part of treatment success.
  • Participation includes attending parent training, supporting intervention strategies, and maintaining consistent communication with the treatment team.
  • A parent or designated adult over 18 must be present during all in-home sessions unless otherwise agreed.
Discharge or Termination of Services

ABA services may be discontinued under any of the following circumstances:

  • Parent/guardian requests to end services.
  • Treatment goals have been met or ABA is no longer clinically indicated.
  • Insurance changes, non-payment, or loss of coverage.
  • Repeated cancellations, no-shows, or lack of participation (e.g., more than 3 unexcused cancellations in a month).
  • Safety concerns for staff or child.
  • Disagreement or non-compliance with the agreed treatment plan without attempts to resolve concerns.
Risks and Benefits of ABA

Potential Benefits:

  • Increased communication and social skills
  • Decreased challenging behaviors
  • Improved independence and daily living skills

Potential Risks:

  • Emotional distress during learning
  • Temporary increase in problem behavior
  • Varying progress based on individual needs
Confidentiality

NY Special Care complies with HIPAA and BACB standards. Client records and treatment information will be kept confidential and shared only with authorized individuals or entities. Written consent is required for the release of information to third parties, such as schools or other service providers.

Acknowledgment and Consent

By signing below, I acknowledge that I have read and understand this agreement. I consent to:

  • The provision of ABA services by NY Special Care Inc.
  • The use of my insurance to submit claims on behalf of the client
  • Adherence to NY Special Care’s treatment, communication, and discharge policies
  • Communication via email, phone, or text unless I request paper-based communication
